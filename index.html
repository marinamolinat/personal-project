What's Cybersecurity?

As the name states, cybersecurity is a field that focuses on security in networks and computer systems. This includes our beloved phones, computers, accounts, and personal information. Even if the use of technology has exponentially increased, awareness of cybersecurity hasn't kept up. People are often misinformed and too naive.

(Not so) FUN FACT: Every 39 seconds, a hacker attack occurs. Its estimated that 1 in 3 americans are affected yearly because of poor cybersecurity

People are dumb

Just kidding. They actually aren't, just misinformed. We are rarely taught about cybersecurity, the idea of cybersecurity in the media is largely distorted. Movies and shows portray it inaccurately, making it seem as something incomprehensible, largely complex, and completely abstract. Even if cybersecurity is no easy field, its basics can be learned easily. If awareness is created, less people will fall victim to attacks.

  • Cybercrime might cost business $10.5 trillion by 2025
  • Every minute, more than 3 companies will be victim to ransomware attacks. Don't know what a ransomware attack is? You should.
  • 3.4 billion phishing emails are sent daily. Roughly 100 million phishing scams are blocked by Google every day. Mine was :(. More on that later though
  • Even though only hald of small business have a cybersecurity plan in place, they acccount for 43% of all cyber attacks.

*Panics* What now?

Firstly, the simple solution: to teach and educate others about cybersecurity. We are often taught about how to stay safe at parties, in solitude dark streets, plane crashes, in fires, dog fights, etc. Yet falling victim to a cyberattack can be more likely than a lot of those, and we are rarely taught about hat. Yes, sometimes we are reminded to have strong passwords and to not fall for online scams, however there are much information that should still be taught: Such as phishing sites, keyloggers, breaches, etc.
